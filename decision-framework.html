Stick with Off-the-Shelf

For your current situation, off-the-shelf software makes more sense. You might reconsider custom when:

  • Your software costs exceed $20K/year
  • You develop unique processes that differentiate you
  • Integration becomes mission-critical
  • You're ready to scale significantly
OR

Borderline Case - Consider These Factors

Custom software could work, but evaluate non-financial factors:

  • Competitive advantage: Will custom software give you an edge?
  • Future growth: Will you outgrow SaaS pricing soon?
  • Team capacity: Can you dedicate time to the project?
  • Risk tolerance: Comfortable with upfront investment?

If most of these are "yes," lean toward custom. If "no," stick with off-the-shelf for now.

OR

Strong Case for Custom Software

The numbers and your situation point to custom software. Here's why:

  • Financial sense: ROI timeline is favorable
  • Competitive edge: Proprietary platform creates moat
  • Ownership: You control the roadmap and costs
  • Scalability: Grows with you, not against you
  • Integration: Everything works together seamlessly

Next step: Calculate your ROI and schedule a consultation.

üí° Pro Tip: The Hybrid Approach

Often the best answer is BOTH. Use off-the-shelf for commodity functions (email, accounting, communication) and custom for your core competitive advantages (unique workflows, proprietary processes, integration layer). This gives you the best of both worlds.
